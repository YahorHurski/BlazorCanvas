  <what-built>
    The three verbs, complete. Clicking a figure selects it with a red 2px outline; clicking empty
    canvas deselects; the topmost figure wins an overlap. A press that moves less than 3px is a click
    that writes nothing; 3px or more is a drag that clamps per-axis at the canvas edge, slides along it,
    and commits exactly one UPDATE on drop. Releasing outside the window or Alt-Tabbing mid-drag commits
    at the clamped position. The Delete button is inert until something is selected and removes the
    figure and its row when clicked. A figure that no longer exists in the database disappears silently
    on the next drag.

    Automated coverage already passing: `FigureStoreTests` pins the affected-row-count contract, the
    zero-row-is-not-an-error rule, the circle's survival of a translation, and the two cross-user
    isolation guarantees. `ClampTests` (Phase 1) pins per-axis independence, the inclusive bounds and the
    degenerate cases of the clamp maths itself.

    What follows is only what no test can see.
  </what-built>

  <how-to-verify>
    Setup: `docker compose up -d`, then `dotnet run --project src/BlazorCanvas`. Log in and draw a
    handful of figures to work with — at least two overlapping rectangles, one circle, and one line.

    **Criterion 1 — selection**
    1. With the pointer tool armed (it is armed on load), click a rectangle. It gets a RED outline. Every
       other figure stays black on white. The outline is the same thickness as before — only the colour
       changed.
    2. Click a different figure. The first goes back to black; only one figure is ever red.
    3. Click bare canvas. Nothing is red.
    4. Click where two rectangles OVERLAP. The one drawn LAST (the one visually on top) is the one that
       turns red — not the one underneath.
    5. Click a circle, then a line. Both can be selected. (A line is one pixel thin and needs a precise
       click — that is D-32's known, accepted cost, not a bug.)
    6. Right-click a figure. The selection does NOT change.

    **Criterion 2 — click vs drag, at 3px**
    7. Press a figure and release WITHOUT moving. It turns red and does not move even slightly.
    8. Press a figure and wobble 1-2px before releasing. It still must not move — not visibly, not
       temporarily, and not "move then snap back". Check the database: `docker compose exec -T postgres
       psql -U postgres -d canvas -c "select id,x1,y1,x2,y2 from figures order by id;"` — no row changed.
    9. Drag a figure clearly (well past 3px). It moves, and it is red while and after moving.
    10. Immediately after a drop, without clicking anything else, click Delete. The figure is deleted —
        proving it stayed selected through the drop. This is the whole point of the "stays selected"
        rule.

    **Criterion 3 — the edge clamp, and one UPDATE per drag**
    11. Drag a rectangle hard against the RIGHT edge and keep pushing right while ALSO moving the cursor
        up and down. The rectangle stays pinned at the edge and SLIDES FREELY up and down. If it sticks
        in place, or only moves once you back away from the edge, STOP — that is a blocker.
    12. While doing item 11, watch the rectangle's SIZE. It must not grow or shrink, not by a pixel. If
        it changes size, STOP — the delta clamp was bypassed and coordinates are being clamped
        individually. This is the landmine of the phase and it is a blocker.
    13. Repeat item 11 against the LEFT, TOP and BOTTOM edges.
    14. Drag a CIRCLE hard into a corner and push. It must stay a circle and must not error. If the page
        shows an error or the circuit drops, STOP — a circle failing here is loud on purpose (D-22).
    15. Drop a figure at a specific spot. It lands exactly where you released it, not offset.
    16. Query the database before releasing a drag: the row still holds the OLD coordinates — no
        intermediate write happened. Release, query again: exactly one row changed. Postgres sees exactly
        ONE UPDATE per drag.

    **Criterion 4 — interruption**
    17. Start a drag and, still holding the button, move the cursor off the canvas onto the TOOLBAR and
        keep moving. The figure keeps following. (This is why the handlers are on a page-spanning
        wrapper.)
    18. Start a drag and move the cursor onto the grey page background beside the canvas. Still follows.
    19. Start a drag and move the cursor entirely OUT of the browser window, then release outside it.
        Come back. The figure is committed at its clamped position — not stuck to the cursor, and it did
        not jump anywhere on the way.
    20. Start a drag and Alt-Tab away. Release the button in the other window. Alt-Tab back and move the
        cursor WITHOUT pressing. The figure is committed and does NOT follow the cursor. If it is welded
        to the cursor, STOP — that is a blocker.
    21. After items 19 and 20, press F5. The figure is where it was committed.

    **Criterion 5 — delete**
    22. On load with nothing selected, the Delete button is grey. Clicking it does nothing. Hovering
        gives no hover treatment.
    23. Select a figure. The Delete button becomes active. Click it. The figure disappears immediately —
        no confirmation dialog appears, and none should (that was ratified deliberately: there is no
        undo, so this is permanent). The button greys out again.
    24. Confirm the row is gone: `docker compose exec -T postgres psql -U postgres -d canvas -c "select
        id from figures order by id;"`.
    25. Select a figure and press the DELETE KEY on the keyboard. NOTHING happens. This is correct and
        deliberate (D-33) — there is no Delete-key handler and there must not be one.
    26. Press F5 after several moves and at least one deletion. Every move and every deletion is still
        there.

    **Regression — Phase 3 must still work**
    27. Arm the RECTANGLE tool. Press INSIDE an existing figure and drag. A NEW rectangle is drawn on top
        — the figure underneath does NOT move and does NOT get selected. If pressing a figure with a
        shape tool armed grabs it instead of drawing, STOP — that is a blocker, and it means the
        conditional press-claim is hardcoded.
    28. Draw one of each of the four shapes again. All still draw and persist.
    29. Arm a shape tool: the cursor is a crosshair everywhere, including over existing figures. Arm the
        pointer: hovering a figure shows a grab (open hand) cursor, bare canvas does not. Hold and drag:
        the cursor becomes grabbing (closed hand) and stays that way while over the figure.

    **Cross-user isolation**
    30. Log out. Log in as a different new username. The canvas is EMPTY. Draw one figure, drag it,
        delete it — all work. Log back in as the first user: their figures are untouched and exactly as
        left.

    Report anything that differs. These are blockers, each because a locked decision would be failing:
    item 11 (sticks instead of slides), item 12 (resizes instead of moves), item 20 (welded to the
    cursor after Alt-Tab), item 25 (the Delete key doing something), item 27 (a shape tool grabbing
    instead of drawing), and item 30 (seeing another user's figures).

    Item 8 is the subtle one worth extra attention: "moves then snaps back" is a FAIL, not a pass. D-48
    exists to stop a hand-wobble from nudging the figure at all.
  </how-to-verify>
