    The whole app now runs on the D-59 anchor + geometry storage model. Concretely, since v1.1:
    the canvas-edge clamp is gone (figures may be drawn and dragged off the canvas, and the circle no
    longer shrinks near an edge); a drag writes only the two anchor columns and can no longer alter a

    6. Press F5. Confirm every remaining figure is exactly where you left it.

    C. The diagonal-line landmine (ROADMAP SC3)
    7. Arm the line tool and drag a diagonal from the BOTTOM-LEFT to the TOP-RIGHT.
    8. Press F5. Confirm the line still runs bottom-left to top-right. If it comes back running

       about because nothing errors when it happens.

    9. Drag an existing figure off the right edge until most of it is outside the canvas, and release.
       Confirm it stays partly outside rather than sliding back. Repeat off the left, top and bottom
       edges with different figures. NOTE: an off-canvas figure is currently unrecoverable by design
       (STOR-F1) — use throwaway figures.
    10. Arm the circle tool, press about 10 pixels from the left edge, and drag out about 200 pixels.
        Confirm you get a large circle whose left side is cut off by the canvas edge, NOT a tiny circle.
        Before this phase it was a tiny circle.
    11. Arm each of the four tools in turn and CLICK without dragging. Confirm no figure appears and no

    E. Live cross-tab sync (ROADMAP SC4)
    12. Open the app in a second browser window, logged in as the SAME user, and place the two windows
        side by side.
    13. Draw a shape in window 1. Confirm it appears in window 2 with the same shape and position.
    14. Drag that figure slowly around in window 1. Confirm it GLIDES in window 2 in real time, and that
        when you release, window 2 shows it in exactly the final position with no visible jump or
        twitch at the end.
    15. Delete a figure in window 1. Confirm it disappears from window 2.
    16. Now the resurrection check: delete a figure in window 2, then in window 1 (which may still be
        showing it) drag that same figure. Confirm the figure does NOT come back in window 2.
    17. Now the mid-drag rule: start dragging a figure in window 1 and HOLD the button down. While
        holding, draw a new shape in window 2. Confirm window 1 shows nothing new while you are still
        dragging. Release, then press F5 in window 1 and confirm the new shape is there. (Losing it
        until a refresh is the accepted cost recorded in D-54.)

    F. Save failure and rollback (ROADMAP SC4) — run this LAST, it stops the database
    18. Note where a figure is. Start dragging it, and while still holding, run

    19. Confirm a dialog appears saying the change could not be saved and the canvas will be reloaded.
    20. Run `docker compose start`, wait a few seconds, then click OK in the dialog. Confirm the canvas
        reloads and the figure is back at the position it had BEFORE the failed drag, in both windows.
